1876 - The April Epic




The April Uprising of 1876 broke out prematurely on April 20 in the old style in Koprivshtitsa and was
organized by the Giurgiu Revolutionary Committee. Although unsuccessful, it is a kind of peak of the
Bulgarian national liberation movement. Today's estimates are that the uprising, although unsuccessful,
has achieved another major goal, the political one, as the popular uprising for independence resonates
strongly in Western Europe and Russia. The uprising was prepared at a distance of only 1-2 months,
which explains the failures in the organization and its course. The decision to revolt was taken by
individual BRCC activists. It is not supported by any external force and is ultimately the result of the
efforts of several apostles. More serious preparations for the revolt were made only in the IV and I
districts. The uprising was suppressed by brutal massacres, torture and looting. This is causing outrage in
Europe and America. An international commission of inquiry has been convened in Constantinople.
Great personalities such as Victor Hugo and Dostoevsky raised their voices in defense of the Bulgarians.
The April Uprising of 1876 deepened the Eastern Crisis, and its success was in its international resonance,
achieved with the heroism of thousands of victims. Soon after the uprising, the Russian-Turkish Liberation War broke out.
