1885 - Unification of Bulgaria




The unification of Bulgaria was the de facto unification of the Principality of Bulgaria and Eastern Rumelia in the autumn of 1885. It was coordinated by the
Bulgarian Secret Central Revolutionary Committee (BTCRC). The unification took place after riots in various cities in Eastern Rumelia, followed by a
military coup on September 6 (September 18 in a new style) 1885, supported by the Bulgarian Prince Alexander I and led by Major Danail Nikolaev.
On the night of September 6, troops commanded by Major Nikolaev established control over Plovdiv and, without resistance, overthrew the government and
Governor-General Gavril Krastevich. The Provisional Government, which includes representatives of the army of both southern Bulgarian parties, led by
Georgi Stranski, announced a general mobilization of men aged 18 to 40 to resist the expected Turkish attack and invited Prince Alexander Battenberg to
support the Union.The events in Plovdiv provoked mass demonstrations in support of the Principality. Faced with a fait accompli, Karavelov set out to establish
the Union in cooperation with the prince. On September 8, Alexander I issued a proclamation declaring himself "Prince of Northern and Southern Bulgaria",
and the next day he was in Plovdiv and appointed three commissioners to replace the interim government, which relinquished its powers. Meanwhile,
the princely government mobilized its army and sent its available troops in support of the militia in southern Bulgaria.
